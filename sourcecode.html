Computer Network

What is a Computer Network?

A computer network is a group of interconnected computers that communicate with each other to share resources, data, and applications. These networks can be as small as two devices or as large as millions of devices spread across the globe.

Types of Computer Networks

  • LAN (Local Area Network): A network that connects computers in a small, localized area, such as a home, office, or campus.
  • WAN (Wide Area Network): A network that spans a large geographic area, such as the Internet.
  • MAN (Metropolitan Area Network): A network that covers a city or a large campus.
  • PAN (Personal Area Network): A network for personal devices, typically within a range of a few meters.

Importance of Computer Networks

Computer networks play a crucial role in today's world, enabling communication, resource sharing, and access to information. They form the backbone of the Internet, powering industries, businesses, and personal communications.
